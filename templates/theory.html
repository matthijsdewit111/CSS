Theoretical background



Diffusion-limited aggregation (DLA)

T.A. Witten & L.M. Sander (1981)

  • Particles undergo a random walk due to Brownian motion
  • Cluster together and form aggregates
  • Applicable to aggregation in any system where diffusion is the primary means of transport




Dendritic branching

Ramón y Cajal (1888)

  • Multi-step biological process: neurons form new dendritic branches
  • Varying morphology depending on function (type) of the neuron
  • Extent to which intrinsic and extrinsic factors shape dendritic geometry remains largely unknown (Scott and Luo, 2001)
  • Complex interactions between different factors during development → difficult to separate their contributions experimentally

Modelling dendrites with DLA

A. Luczak (2006)

  • Computationally model dendritic development
  • Reproduce spatial embedding of different types of neurons
  • External factors: space available for growth & spatial distribution of ‘neurotrophic particles’ (NPs)
  • Conclusion: Succesfully created complex and diverse 3D trees strictly similar to observed neuronal shapes (no intrinsic factors needed)
  • (WHAT WE COULD DO: change only intrinsic factor (pruning span) & see if this can also be sufficient to reproduce shapes)




Research question & hypothesis


    → To what extent can various types of neuronal dendrite be simulated by pruning of a diffusion-limited aggregation model?
